Frederick Hobbes was standing in a steel lined, vaulted room. The faint odor of carbonic acid was still tingling in his nostrils from the day's previous occurrences. He sighed heavily, wishing only for the day to be over, yet at the same time recognizing that a new day would then begin.


He sat, in as existential a manner as possible, upon the bare cold metal floor. Even through his well insulated pants the death-like chill of the alloyed material made itself known. He sat, thinking of all that had happened previously.
